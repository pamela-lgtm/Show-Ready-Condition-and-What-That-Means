The Show Ready Standard.
Why it changes everything.

Before a single tenant walks through your door, before a single photo is taken, before a single day of marketing begins — your property must be show ready. This is not a preference. It is the foundation of every great outcome we deliver.

Verandah Properties manages a curated portfolio of rental homes in the Lake Nona and St. Cloud corridor. Every property we represent carries our name — and our name stands for a standard. Show ready is not the last step before listing. It is the first step toward the outcome you deserve.

Why Show Ready Is the Most Important Step of All

The rental market is a visual, emotional, and competitive experience for tenants. The best applicants — the ones with strong credit, stable income, excellent rental history, and genuine respect for a home — have options. They are comparing your property to others. They make their decision quickly. And the condition of a property at the time of showing is the single most powerful factor in whether they apply or move on.

01
First impressions are permanent — and they happen online
Before a tenant ever sets foot in your home, they have already judged it. Professional photos of a show-ready property create the emotional connection that drives applications. Photos of an unprepared property drive the scroll.
02
Qualified tenants self-select based on what they see
A well-presented home attracts applicants who take pride in where they live. A neglected or unready home attracts applicants who either don't notice or don't care — and neither of those is the tenant you want.
03
The condition of the property communicates your standards
When you present a home with care and attention to detail, you are telling every prospective tenant: this is how this home is maintained. That signal attracts tenants who will maintain it the same way.

What Show Ready Actually Means

Show ready means a prospective tenant can walk through your property and genuinely picture themselves moving in that day. Not next month after some work is done. Not after the carpets are cleaned. That day. Every detail communicates something — and the details we ask you to address are not arbitrary. Each one directly impacts the quality of tenant who applies.

🏠

Interior — Every Room, Every Detail

Non-Negotiable
Cleanliness
  • Professional deep clean throughout — not a tidy, a thorough professional clean. Every surface, every corner, every appliance inside and out.
  • All personal items and belongings completely removed from the home and garage
  • All walls, doors, and baseboards wiped down — pay attention to light switches, hallways, and doorframes
  • All ceiling fans and light fixtures cleaned — dust accumulation shows immediately in photos
  • All windows, window sills, and blinds cleaned — screens secured and intact
  • All bathrooms cleaned and disinfected to a hotel standard
  • New A/C filter installed — a small detail that signals attentive maintenance
Flooring
  • Carpet professionally cleaned if in good condition — or replaced if worn, stained, or more than a few years old
  • All tile and vinyl mopped and free of stains and scuff marks
  • Hard flooring with minimal wear and tear — no excessive scratching
  • Wood flooring with clean grout lines and no deep gouges or damage
Paint & Walls
  • Fresh neutral paint throughout — bold or personal color choices limit the tenant's ability to see themselves in the space
  • No scuffs, marks, nail holes, or visible touch-up patches
  • Consistent finish — no mismatched paint sheens between walls and trim
Kitchen & Bathrooms
  • Kitchen and bathrooms updated within the past 5 years — or presenting fixtures and finishes that feel current and well-maintained
  • All appliances clean, functioning, and matching in color — or stainless steel
  • No dripping faucets, running toilets, or broken hardware of any kind
  • Updated light fixtures and plumbing fixtures — dated brass or builder-basic hardware reads as neglect to a qualified tenant
  • Clean grout lines in tile — discolored or cracked grout is one of the most common reasons tenants pass on a property
General Functionality
  • All fixtures, doors, locks, and windows functioning as intended — no sticky doors, broken locks, or jammed windows
  • All light bulbs working — every single one. A dark room in a showing reads as a problem
  • Window blinds installed, clean, and fully operational throughout
  • All noticeable damages repaired or replaced — we assess what matters most during our walk-through
  • Garage clean and free of personal items — garage space is a major selling point and should look like one
🌿

Exterior — The First Thing They See

Non-Negotiable
Lawn & Landscaping
  • Lawn mowed, trimmed, and edged — a clean lawn line signals that this property is cared for
  • Irrigation set to automatic — the lawn should be actively maintained, not just cut once
  • All beds weeded and fresh mulch applied where appropriate
  • Dead or overgrown plants removed or replaced — a neglected landscape is the first impression and it sets the tone for everything that follows
Exterior Surfaces
  • Power wash all exterior surfaces if needed — in Florida, mold and mildew accumulate quickly and show prominently in photos
  • Driveway and walkways clean and free of oil stains or debris
  • Repair or replace any damaged or missing screens — for all windows, porches, or pool enclosures
  • Check for and repair any trip hazards on walkways or entry areas
Pool & Patio
  • Pool clean, balanced, and blue — a green pool is a deal-breaker. Period.
  • All patio and outdoor furniture removed or neatly arranged — no personal items, no clutter
  • Screened enclosure clean and free of tears or damage
  • All outdoor personal items and trash fully removed from the property
Final Handover Details
  • All manuals, extra keys (labeled), gate cards, pool keys, recreation cards, and remotes left on the kitchen counter for Verandah
  • Provide all keys needed to enter the property — including any community access cards, garage remotes, mailbox keys, and amenity keys
  • All utilities active in owner's name while vacant — electricity and water must be on for showings
The Consequence of Not Being Show Ready

This is not about perfectionism. It is about outcomes. Here is what the data and experience tell us about what happens when a property is listed before it is truly show ready — versus what happens when it is.

🔴 Not Show Ready — What Happens
  • ⬇Qualified tenants view the property and pass — they move on to a better-presented option
  • ⬇Extended days on market — Zillow's algorithm deprioritizes listings that sit
  • ⬇Price reductions required to attract applicants who overlook condition
  • ⬇Lower-quality applicant pool — those with fewer options accept what qualified tenants reject
  • ⬇Higher risk of maintenance issues, lease disputes, and difficult tenancy outcomes
  • ⬇Your investment is not protected — it is exposed
✅ Show Ready — What Happens
  • ⬆Strong qualified applicant pool from day one — the first 7–10 days drive the best traffic
  • ⬆Faster lease-up — a show-ready property leases 2–3x faster than an unprepared one
  • ⬆Full asking rent achieved — qualified tenants pay the price; desperate tenants negotiate
  • ⬆Higher-caliber tenant who maintains the property at the standard it was presented
  • ⬆Lower maintenance calls and incidents throughout the tenancy
  • ⬆Your investment is actively protected — from day one
⚠ The Cost of Vacancy Days

Every day a property sits vacant because it was not ready to show is a day of rental income that cannot be recovered. On a $2,500/month property, each day of unnecessary vacancy costs approximately $83. Preparing the property properly before listing is almost always the faster and more financially sound path — not the slower one.

Your Responsibility. Our Support.

The show-ready standard is the owner's responsibility — and we are straightforward about that from the beginning. You know your property, you have access to it, and you are best positioned to get it prepared. What we bring is guidance, clarity, and a trusted vendor network if you need it.

If you need help finding reliable vendors — cleaners, painters, flooring specialists, landscapers, handymen — we are happy to connect you with the professionals in our network who know our standard. We do not manage the work on your behalf during the preparation phase, but we make sure you are never left trying to figure it out alone.

✅ How the Confirmation Process Works

Once you believe the property is show-ready, our New Client Liaison will visit the property in person to confirm it meets our standard before photography is ordered and the listing goes live. If everything is in order — we move immediately. If there is something minor outstanding — a final clean, a carpet clean, a small touch-up — we will take care of it on your behalf prior to move-in so the property is presented at its absolute best. The goal is always a seamless, fast transition from ready to rented.
